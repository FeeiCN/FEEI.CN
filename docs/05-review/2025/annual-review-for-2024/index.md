## 🏃 Health and Strong Body

**The Malignant Bladder Cancer in My Grandmother Has Been Cured** . My grandmother started bleeding from her urine in February, but she didn’t tell anyone. When the situation became severe, my uncles took her to a county hospital for an examination. The doctor said that a cystoscopy was needed to determine if the tumor was malignant or benign. Upon hearing this, my uncles thought it was too risky and too dangerous because of her age, so they took her to a town clinic where she received saline drips and medications to stop the bleeding.

In early May, when I learned about her condition, I felt like she was just waiting for death to come, so I immediately went back home and took my grandmother to Hangzhou for further examination at Zhejiang Fist Hospital. She was diagnosed with a malignant tumor, and after discussing the consequences with the doctors, I called an emergency family meeting and convinced everyone to support the surgery.

In late May, she successfully underwent surgery and was discharged from the hospital. Though it’s been less than a month, I feel like I’ve run to the hospital countless times, filled out endless forms, and stayed up through too many sleepless nights. Following her discharge, she underwent two months of follow-up examinations and recuperation and is now largely able to take care of herself.

My grandmother is a kind and devoted Buddhist, and I spent a significant portion of my childhood living with her. A large part of who I am today comes from her teachings, especially her lessons in kindness. When the time comes to repay her kindness, I will do so without hesitation. This is the second time I have encountered a family member with cancer. I have properly configured insurance coverage and take responsibility for your health. Life is short and unpredictable, so live in the moment.
